About Me

I earned a Bachelor of Science in Geology from the University of Calgary, where I became intrigued by the vast amount of data in the field. In my final year, I started questioning how large datasets could be used to interpret complex geological concepts, which piqued my interest in integrating data analysis.

To explore this further, I took an introductory course in data science and quickly realized my passion for it. Graduating in 2020 during the challenges of COVID-19 and the downturn in the oil and gas industry, I saw an opportunity to pivot into data analytics. I became more engaged by participating in data-focused hackathons, gaining hands-on experience with real-world data and collaborating with professionals. These experiences not only strengthened my technical abilities but also allowed me to expand my network with like-minded individuals. Through this transition, I became an active volunteer with organizations such as the YYC Data Society, contributing to the data community and furthering my growth in the field.

This led to my transition from working as an Environmental Lab Assistant to securing my first role as a Business Analyst at a startup. Shortly after, I landed a role as a Business Intelligence Analyst in the tech industry, where I gained real-world experience in data analysis, honing both my technical and soft skills to support business operations.

I am passionate to continue growing and developing my expertise in advanced data analytics, AI integration, and business intelligence. My goal is to leverage data-driven insights to provide well-informed, optimized, and cost-effective business solutions that drive impactful decision-making.
